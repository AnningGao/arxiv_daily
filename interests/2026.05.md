*Generated 2026-05-23 from a topic survey of the Zotero library (~70 collections, single user library). Item counts are approximate collection-level tallies — there is genuine cross-collection overlap (the same Lyman-α paper appears under Cosmology, IGM, and Dark Matter), so the numbers indicate emphasis, not a strict census. `dateAdded` is intentionally downweighted because most items share a migration timestamp from an EndNote→Zotero move; genuinely recent additions (2024–2026) are used only as a secondary "current direction" signal.*

## Executive profile

This is, at its core, a **Lyman-α forest cosmology** library. Almost everything radiates out from one organizing question: *how do you extract cosmological and astrophysical information from the high-redshift intergalactic medium as seen in quasar absorption spectra* — and, increasingly, *how do you do it with modern statistical/machine-learning machinery on DESI-scale data*. The owner is an observational/data-driven cosmologist (not a pure theorist and not an instrument builder) who works at the meeting point of large-scale-structure cosmology, IGM/reionization astrophysics, fundamental-physics constraints (dark matter, neutrinos), and inference methodology.

Three signals point the same way. First, **size**: the Lyman-α and IGM branches are the most heavily sub-divided in the whole library (P1D, P3D, measurements, techniques, theory & simulation, reionization, opacity, UV background, QSO continuum). Second, **centrality**: Lyman-α is the connective tissue that links the cosmology, IGM, and dark-matter branches — it is the topic that shows up *everywhere*. Third, **recency**: the genuinely new additions are DESI DR1/DR2 Lyman-α and BAO results, the effective-field-theory treatment of the forest, and a fast-growing cluster of machine-learning papers (emulators, simulation-based inference, and astronomical foundation models). The trajectory is clearly **Lyman-α × DESI × machine learning converging**.

## 1. Lyman-α forest cosmology — *primary focus*

The flagship topic. The collection is organized exactly the way a working forest cosmologist would organize it: by **statistic** (1D power spectrum `P1D`, 3D power spectrum `P3D`, correlation functions), by **estimator/technique**, by **measurement vs. theory/simulation**, and by **application** (cosmological parameters, dark matter, reionization).

**Scope & sub-themes:**
- **1D flux power spectrum (P1D)** — the dominant sub-theme, traced from the SDSS/BOSS era to DESI. Representative: *"The one-dimensional Lyα forest power spectrum from BOSS"* (2013), *"The one-dimensional power spectrum from the SDSS DR14 Lyα forests"* (2019), *"The Lyman α forest power spectrum from the XQ-100 Legacy Survey"* (2017), and the current DESI generation: *"Cosmological analysis of the DESI DR1 Lyman alpha 1D power spectrum"* (2026), *"DESI DR1 Lyα 1D power spectrum: the optimal estimator measurement"* (2025), and *"DESI DR1 Lyα 1D power spectrum: the Fast Fourier Transform estimator measurement"* (2025).
- **Estimator methodology** — clear interest in *how* P1D is measured (optimal/quadratic vs. FFT estimators), e.g. *"The Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument: one-dimensional power spectrum from first Ly α forest samples with Fast Fourier Transform"* (2023).
- **Compression, emulation & theory** — *"Compressing the Cosmological Information in One-dimensional Correlations of the Lyman-α Forest"* (2023), *"ForestFlow: predicting the Lyman-α forest clustering from linear to nonlinear scales"* (2025), *"Analytic compression of the effective field theory of the Lyman-alpha forest"* (2026), and synthetic-spectrum generation, *"Generation of Lognormal Synthetic Lyman-α Forest Spectra for P1D Analysis"* (2025).
- **Small-scale / high-resolution forest** — *"Small-scale Lyman alpha forest cosmology with PRIYA: Constraints from XQ100 and KODIAQ-SQUAD one-dimensional flux power spectra"* (2025).
- **Fundamental physics via the forest** — neutrino mass (*"Neutrino masses and cosmology with Lyman-alpha forest power spectrum"*, 2015) and dark matter (see §5).

**Activity level: very high and current.** The newest, post-migration additions are dominated by DESI DR1 P1D analyses and the forest EFT — this is plainly the owner's live working area.

## 2. Intergalactic medium & reionization — *primary focus (fused with #1)*

The IGM branch is essentially the *astrophysics half* of the forest program: to use the forest as a cosmological probe you must understand the medium producing it. This branch is as large as the Lyman-α branch and is heavily weighted toward **the end of reionization and the post-reionization IGM**.

**Scope & sub-themes:**
- **Opacity, optical depth & mean free path** — the single largest IGM sub-theme. Representative: *"Probing Ultralate Reionization: Direct Measurements of the Mean Free Path over 5 < z < 6"* (Zhu et al., 2023), *"The Lyman-limit photon mean free path at the end of late reionization in the Sherwood-Relics simulations"* (Feron et al., 2024).
- **Late reionization & the neutral fraction** — *"Hydrogen reionization ends by z = 5.3: Lyman-α optical depth measured by the XQR-30 sample"* (Bosman et al., 2022), *"Damping wing-like features in the stacked Ly α forest: Potential neutral hydrogen islands at z < 6"* (Zhu et al., 2024), *"Measurements of the z > 5 Lyman-α forest flux autocorrelation functions from the extended XQR-30 data set"* (Wolfson et al., 2024).
- **UV background, ionizing sources & thermal state** — extragalactic UV/X-ray background and photoionization modelling.
- **QSO continuum reconstruction** — quasar composites/spectral properties, an essential systematic for forest measurements.
- **HI & DLA** — damped Lyman-α systems, neutral hydrogen column densities at z~5.

**Activity level: high.** Recent additions cluster on opacity and late-reionization signatures — consistent with the forest-cosmology focus pushing to the highest accessible redshifts.

## 3. Machine learning & statistical inference for cosmology — *rising primary focus*

This is the fastest-growing part of the library and carries the **strongest recency signal**. It is not generic ML — it is the methodological toolkit a forest/LSS cosmologist needs to do inference on expensive simulations and large spectroscopic datasets.

**Scope & sub-themes:**
- **Simulation-based inference (SBI)** — *"The frontier of simulation-based inference"* (2020), *"A Trust Crisis In Simulation-Based Inference?"* (2021), and *"Low-Budget Simulation-Based Inference with Bayesian Neural Networks"* (2024). Combined with summary-statistic theory, *"How to evaluate the sufficiency and complementarity of summary statistics for cosmic fields"* (2025).
- **Normalizing flows & stochastic interpolants / diffusion** — a notably current sub-cluster: *"Building Normalizing Flows with Stochastic Interpolants"* (2023), *"Stochastic Interpolants: A Unifying Framework for Flows and Diffusions"* (2025), and the cosmology application *"Joint cosmological parameter inference and initial condition reconstruction with Stochastic Interpolants"* (2024); plus *"Neural Spline Flows"* (2019).
- **Neural operators / emulation** — *"Fourier Neural Operator for Parametric Partial Differential Equations"* (2020), *"Learning nonlinear operators via DeepONet"* (2021) — the surrogate-modelling backbone for replacing hydro simulations (cf. ForestFlow in §1).
- **Bayesian deep learning & uncertainty** — *"Simple and Scalable Predictive Uncertainty Estimation using Deep Ensembles"* (2017), *"Dropout as a Bayesian Approximation"* (2016).
- **Sampling** — *"emcee: The MCMC Hammer"* (2013), *"dynesty: a dynamic nested sampling package"* (2020), *"The Ray Tracing Sampler: Bayesian Sampling of Neural Networks for Everyone"* (2025).
- **Symbolic regression** — *"AI Feynman"* (2020), *"A Neural Symbolic Model for Space Physics"* (2025).
- **Astronomical foundation models** — the most recent additions of all: *"AION-1: Omnimodal Foundation Model for Astronomical Sciences"* (the single newest item, added 2026-05), *"AstroCLIP: a cross-modal foundation model for galaxies"* (2024), *"SpecPT (Spectroscopy Pre-trained Transformer)"* (2025), *"Universal Spectral Tokenization via Self-Supervised Panchromatic Representation Learning"* (2025).

**Activity level: rising sharply.** Foundation models and stochastic-interpolant flows are recent and trend-aware. This branch is best read as *methods the owner intends to apply to forest/LSS data*, not as a separate ML research line.

## 4. Large-scale structure cosmology & spectroscopic surveys — *secondary focus*

The "rest of cosmology" context around the forest: the broader LSS probes, the statistical machinery, the simulations, and the surveys that supply the data.

**Scope & sub-themes:**
- **BAO & full-shape clustering** — strong and current, dominated by DESI: *"DESI 2024 IV: Baryon Acoustic Oscillations from the Lyman alpha forest"* (2025) and the recent *"Dynamical dark energy in light of the DESI DR2 baryonic acoustic oscillations measurements"* (2025, one of the newest adds).
- **Void cosmology** — a well-developed secondary probe with its own finders (VIDE/ZOBOV/DIVE), measurements, and modelling; voids-as-dark-energy-probe.
- **Techniques** — covariance estimation, Fisher forecasting, density/halo/void finders, scattering transforms, 2PCF estimators. A mature "precision-cosmology" methods shelf.
- **Simulations & codes** — N-body/hydro codes and large suites (Abacus, Quijote, MultiDark, CAMELS) plus neural emulators — the infrastructure layer feeding both §1 and §3.
- **Surveys** — **DESI dominates** the modern era (overview, EDR, survey validation, target selection, pipeline, data releases); **SDSS/eBOSS/BOSS** form the legacy/methodology foundation; **J-PAS / miniJPAS** and quasar target selection (often ML-based, e.g. *"The miniJPAS survey quasar selection V: combined algorithm"* (2025), *"A gradient boosting and broadband approach to finding Lyman-α emitting galaxies"* (2025), *XGBoost*) round it out. Other probes (CMB, SN, BBN, weak lensing) are present but thin — context rather than focus.

**Activity level: high for DESI BAO/full-shape; evergreen for techniques and simulations.**

## 5. Dark matter from small-scale structure — *secondary focus (fused with #1)*

A focused, physics-driven application of the forest: using the smallest scales of the Lyman-α power spectrum to constrain non-cold dark matter.

**Scope & sub-themes:** warm dark matter (*"Warm dark matter as a solution to the small scale crisis"*, Viel et al. 2013; *"New constraints on warm dark matter from the Lyman-α forest power spectrum"*, Villasenor et al. 2023; *"Lyman-alpha forests cool warm dark matter"*, Baur et al. 2016), free-streaming at the smallest scales (*"Unveiling dark matter free streaming at the smallest scales with the high redshift Lyman-alpha forest"*, Iršič et al. 2024), ultralight/fuzzy dark matter and axions (*"Strong Bound on Canonical Ultralight Axion Dark Matter from the Lyman-Alpha Forest"*, Rogers & Peiris 2021), and the nature-of-DM framing (*"The Lyman-α forest as a diagnostic of the nature of the dark matter"*, 2019). Stellar streams as a DM probe appear only minimally (one item).

**Activity level: steady, with recent free-streaming work.** This is best treated as a sub-branch of the forest program rather than an independent dark-matter-detection interest.

## 6–9. Adjacent & peripheral interests

- **Circumgalactic medium (CGM)** *(adjacent, ~5%)* — metals and cool gas around galaxies: *"The Circumgalactic Medium"* (Tumlinson, Peeples & Werk 2017), *"The COS CGM Compendium"* (Lehner et al. 2018), *"The CGM2 Survey: Circumgalactic O VI..."* (Tchernyshyov et al. 2022), *"KODIAQ-Z..."* (Lehner et al. 2022). Heavily O VI / absorption-line driven — a natural sibling of the IGM interest, viewed through the same quasar-absorption lens.
- **21cm cosmology / cosmic dawn** *(adjacent, ~5%)* — the *other* high-z probe: *"21 cm cosmology in the 21st century"* (Pritchard & Loeb 2012), *"Improved Constraints on the 21 cm EoR Power Spectrum ... with HERA Phase I Observations"* (2023), *"Joint analysis constraints on the physics of the first galaxies with low-frequency radio astronomy data"* (Bevins et al. 2024). Complements the reionization interest from the radio side.
- **Galaxy evolution** *(adjacent, ~5%)* — cosmic star-formation history, JWST high-z galaxies (z>8 UV luminosity functions, GOLDRUSH), and AGN–galaxy coevolution. Context for the ionizing-source and reionization questions.
- **Stellar abundances & dynamics** *(peripheral, ~3%)* — GALAH/APOGEE elemental abundances, exoplanet-host chemistry, and a little N-body/globular-cluster dynamics. The least connected branch; likely legacy or breadth reading.

## Cross-cutting structure & what the recency signal says

The library is best understood as **one program with several faces**, not a set of independent topics. The Lyman-α forest is simultaneously: a *cosmology* probe (P1D/P3D, BAO, neutrino mass — §1, §4), an *IGM/reionization* observable (§2), a *dark-matter* constraint (§5), a *DESI* data product (§4), and increasingly an *ML inference* target (§3). The collection structure splits these for convenience, but they share papers and authors.

The genuine recent additions (the part of `dateAdded` worth trusting) reinforce this: AION-1 (foundation model, newest of all), DESI DR2 dynamical-dark-energy BAO, DESI DR1 Lyα P1D analyses, the EFT of the Lyman-α forest, stochastic-interpolant flows, and late-reionization opacity papers. The forward direction is **applying modern inference (SBI, emulators, flows, foundation models) to DESI-era Lyman-α / LSS data**.

## Recommendation toolkit

### How to weight recommendations
Prioritize, in order: (1) Lyman-α forest (any of P1D/P3D, measurement, estimator, emulation, EFT); (2) IGM opacity / late reionization / mean free path; (3) ML-for-cosmology methods *with a clear inference or emulation angle*; (4) DESI/eBOSS LSS (BAO, full-shape, voids); (5) small-scale dark matter via the forest. A paper hitting **two or more** of these (e.g. "SBI for the Lyman-α P1D from DESI") is a near-certain match.

### Per-topic search keywords / query strings
- **Lyman-α forest:** `Lyman-alpha forest P1D`, `1D flux power spectrum`, `Lyman-alpha P3D`, `forest BAO`, `effective field theory Lyman-alpha forest`, `Lyα emulator`, `quadratic/optimal estimator power spectrum`, `flux decoding / continuum fitting`, `DESI Lyman-alpha`.
- **IGM & reionization:** `IGM opacity`, `Lyman-alpha optical depth z>5`, `mean free path ionizing photons`, `late/patchy reionization`, `neutral hydrogen islands`, `UV background photoionization`, `IGM thermal state / temperature-density relation`, `damping wing`.
- **ML / inference:** `simulation-based inference cosmology`, `neural posterior estimation`, `normalizing flows / stochastic interpolants`, `neural operator emulator`, `field-level inference`, `astronomical foundation model / spectroscopic transformer`, `symbolic regression physics`.
- **LSS & surveys:** `DESI BAO DR2`, `full-shape galaxy power spectrum`, `void size function dark energy`, `covariance estimation power spectrum`, `Fisher forecast`, `quasar target selection`.
- **Dark matter:** `warm dark matter Lyman-alpha`, `fuzzy/ultralight axion dark matter forest`, `free-streaming small-scale power spectrum`, `sterile neutrino structure formation`.

### arXiv categories to monitor
- **Primary:** `astro-ph.CO` (cosmology & nongalactic) — the main feed.
- **Secondary:** `astro-ph.GA` (IGM/CGM/galaxies), `astro-ph.IM` (methods/estimators/pipelines), `cs.LG` + `stat.ML` (ML methods, filtered for inference/generative/emulation), `hep-ph` (dark matter/neutrino constraints).

### Surveys / data sources central to this profile
DESI (top priority), eBOSS/BOSS/SDSS (legacy), XQ-100, KODIAQ/SQUAD, XQR-30 (high-z quasars), HERA/CHIME/SARAS (21cm), J-PAS/miniJPAS; simulation suites Sherwood/Sherwood-Relics, PRIYA, Quijote, CAMELS, Abacus.

### Authors / groups whose new papers are high-value
Recurring or central to these topics: forest/IGM cosmology — Iršič, Viel, Bosman, Zhu, Becker, Feron, Wolfson, Walther, Karaçaylı, Cuceu, Font-Ribera, Palanque-Delabrouille, Yèche, the DESI Lyman-α working group; dark matter via forest — Rogers & Peiris, Murgia; CGM — Tumlinson, Werk, Peeples, Lehner, Tchernyshyov; 21cm — Pritchard, Loeb, Bevins, Fialkov; ML-for-cosmology — Cranmer, Ho, Lanusse, Villaescusa-Navarro, and the SBI/flows community (Wandelt, Alsing-style field-level inference).

### Likely to recommend
- New DESI (and future Lyα-survey, e.g. WEAVE-QSO, PFS) forest power-spectrum and BAO papers.
- Methods papers coupling SBI / normalizing flows / neural emulators to forest or LSS inference.
- High-z IGM opacity, mean-free-path, and reionization-end measurements/simulations.
- Forest-based constraints on WDM, fuzzy DM, axions, neutrino mass.
- Astronomical foundation / spectroscopic models with a cosmology or spectroscopy-analysis angle.

### Probably skip / low priority
- Pure instrumentation/hardware engineering papers (instrument context is thin).
- Solar-system, exoplanet-detection, stellar-interior, or gravitational-wave/compact-object papers.
- Generic computer-vision/NLP ML with no scientific-inference or generative-modelling relevance.
- Strong-lensing, photometric-redshift, and imaging-AI papers (notably absent from the library).

### Adjacent / emerging topics worth surfacing (expansion candidates)
Field-level / differentiable forward modelling of the forest; cross-correlations (Lyα × CMB lensing, Lyα × galaxies/quasars, Lyα × kSZ); machine-learned continuum fitting and contaminant (DLA/BAL) identification; foundation models fine-tuned for spectroscopic survey pipelines; next-generation forest surveys (WEAVE-QSO, PFS, 4MOST); and joint Lyα + 21cm reionization inference.
